Hi, I'm Fernando, an anaesthesiologist with about 10 years of experience and now, I'm learning AI security in healthcare, in public. My training as an anaesthesiologist taught me the importance of staying attentive all the time, to capture information and turn it into action. During the anaesthesia, the patient is completely vulnerable and it's the anaesthesiologist's role to protect the patient's life, and, we need to monitor the blood pressure, heart rate, oxygen saturation, level of consciousness, and a lot of other parameters, continuously, throughout the entire surgery, and some last many hours (my personal record: a 25-hour surgery). We don't accept any information as true, all information needs to be checked, whether from the patient, the surgeon, or the monitors, because our decisions cannot be undone — a drug, once injected, does not come back. Every time a monitor gives us a reading, we check whether it makes sense. In medicine, we have a saying: "the clinic is sovereign." The patient's clinical picture is the only thing that never lies. That discipline is exactly what will be missing when AI becomes deeply embedded in clinical systems. I have no conference talks, no CVEs, no merged OSS contributions; no background in ML research; no formal cryptography; only the basics of adversarial ML, prompt injection, MITRE ATLAS; OWASP Top 10 for LLM read but not yet internalized.

Today is April 18, 2026, and I decided to start this new journey. Honestly, I don't know how much time it will take, but for me, it's not a problem, I'm willing to spend as long as necessary to be really good at it. Two writeups per month, minimum. Quarterly public reviews with honest accounting of what worked and what didn't. I thought about writing out the path I plan to follow, but this can change, so we'll discover it over time. The path goes from someone with simple doubts to someone with complex ones.

On this blog, you'll find the reasoning behind my thinking, records of how it evolves over time, passing through each stage of the process, starting with this about. This is a live record of this physician learning to defend clinical AI — mistakes and all.
